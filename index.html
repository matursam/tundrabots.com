What is FTC?

FIRST Tech Challenge is a robotics competition for high schoolers, designed to be a “varsity sport for the mind”. FTC focuses on creating a team-based, sports-like competition for teenagers interested in STEM fields. As a part of the competition, teams are exposed to mechanical, electrical, and software engineering, as well as marketing and teamwork.
For more information, check out FIRST’s official website, which has many guides explaining all aspects of the competition.

Starting a Team

If you are planning to create a team you may not know where to start. If you are a potential coach you may need to find people to be on the team or be worried about funding. If you are a student who wishes to start a team you will also have to deal with these issues. However it is not as daunting as it sounds robotics is intriguing and. When reaching out to potential members first and foremost you have to really consider whether they would mesh with your other team members. Second then you consider who they are. I know this is strange question but from our experience we have found that there are 5 different types of people in robotics but many often take multiple of these roles or share them. These roles are manager, wiring, team leader, builder and programmer.

The manager manages the engineering notebook and other paperwork this job can also include outreach and fundraising. Wiring is in charge of the control system of the robot they work closely with the builders to integrate the control elements into the design. The team leader is only needed for some teams and really depends on your team dynamic. This job is to direct the overall operations of the team. The builder is a role that is often taken up by a main builder and many other members of the team there job is to manage the physical robot and to build it (duh). The final position is the programer their job is to program the robot this job to manage write the program for the robot. I must emphasize that these are rough and should not be followed to the letter. Another thing to think about is team dynamic this grows naturally but should be considered when adding team members.

Competition Day

When you are at the competition there is an ideal code of conduct that you should try to follow. Courteousness to other teams is of the utmost importance. The nicer you are to other teams, the nicer they will be to you. When a team needs a part or aid repairing their robot, do not hesitate to offer a helping hand. If you are being considered for an award, the judges may be keeping an eye on your team to determine if you truly deserve it. Have a few team members go around and talk to every team that you can, keeping track of their strengths and weaknesses to help you pick a team if you are an alliance captain. The competitions are fun and exciting events, and we wish you luck!
